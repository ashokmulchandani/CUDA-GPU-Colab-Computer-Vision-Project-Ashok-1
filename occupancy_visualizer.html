🧵 Is block always 16×16? NO — it is YOUR choice!
Common block sizes: 16×16=256 (most common, good occupancy) | 32×8=256 (same threads, different shape) | 8×8=64 (simple kernels) | 32×32=1024 (max per block)
Why 16×16? 256 threads = 8 warps. Fits nicely in SM. 16 is half of 32 (warp size) — good for 2D problems.
Rule: Threads per block should be a multiple of 32 (warp size). 128, 256, 512, 1024 are the sweet spots.
T4 Limits: Max 64 warps/SM | Max 32 blocks/SM | 65,536 registers/SM | 64 KB shared memory/SM
Occupancy = min(block_limit, register_limit, shared_memory_limit, warp_limit) / 64
