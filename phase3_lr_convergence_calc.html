Learning Rate → Epochs Estimator

How many epochs to reach 90% accuracy on MNIST? Pick a learning rate — the math estimates the rest.

🧠 The Key Relationship (Exponential Decay)

Each epoch, the accuracy gap shrinks by roughly the same factor:   gap_next ≈ gap_current × |1 − 2 × lr| Number of epochs to reach target accuracy:   N ≈ log(G_target / G_start) / log(|1 − 2×lr|) Learning rate for a desired number of epochs:   lr ≈ (1 − (G_target/G_start)^(1/N)) / 2 G_start = accuracy gap at epoch 1 (≈ 0.89 for MNIST starting at 11%) G_target = acceptable gap to 100% (≈ 0.05 for 95% accuracy)

💡 Rules of Thumb for MLP on MNIST (gap 0.89 → 0.05):
  • lr = 0.05 → ~4 epochs (⚡ blazing — may overshoot or be unstable)
  • lr = 0.02 → ~10 epochs (⚡ fast — good if you're in a hurry)
  • lr = 0.01 → ~20 epochs (✅ balanced — our actual training used this, reached 95.55%)
  • lr = 0.005 → ~40 epochs (🐢 smooth — lower risk of instability)
  • lr = 0.001 → ~200 epochs (💤 painfully slow — 10× more training time)
  • lr ≥ 0.5 → ❌ DIVERGES — |1−2×lr| ≥ 1, accuracy gap never shrinks

⚠️ Why target 95% and not 100%? MNIST has inherent noise (bad handwriting, ambiguous digits). Even humans get ~98% on MNIST. Pushing past 98% means overfitting to noise. Our MLP achieved 95.55% — excellent for a 3-layer network with no convolutions.
