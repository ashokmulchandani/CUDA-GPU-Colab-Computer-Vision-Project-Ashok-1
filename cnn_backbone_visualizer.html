What Are Channels? — Just Like RGB, But for PATTERNS Instead of Colors
RGB (3 channels) — You KNOW this
R channel
G channel
B channel
3 grids. Each: "how much of this COLOR here?"
=
16 channels — Same idea, different "question"
Ch 0: vertical edges
Ch 1: horizontal edges
Ch 2: diagonal strokes
Each grid: "how much of this PATTERN here?"
=
256 channels — Abstract object parts
Ch 0: wheel-ness
Ch 1: face-ness
Ch 2: window-ness
Ch 3: door-ness
Each grid: "how much of this OBJECT PART here?"
RGB: 3 numbers per pixel = "this pixel is orange"
16 channels: 16 numbers per pixel = "vertical edge here, no horizontal, diagonal here..."
256 channels: 256 numbers per pixel = "no wheel, no face, window-like here, door-like here..."

Channels grow = more questions asked per pixel. Spatial shrinks = fewer pixels, each richer. Same total information.

Formula: params = out_channels × (filter_h × filter_w × in_channels + 1)
= out_channels × (kernel_weights + 1 bias per filter)

Most of the 3.15M params are in the DEEP layers (L3-L5) where channels are large.
A 3×3 conv from 256→512 channels = 1.18 million parameters alone!
(Plus multiple conv layers per level, 1×1 convs, neck, and head = ~3.15M total)
